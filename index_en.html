Gaugenix

Gaugenix is a system that captures images of gauges using IoT devices and retrieves their values through AI analysis. It automates the manual gauge reading process in factories and plants, improving data collection efficiency and reducing human errors.

For example, cameras regularly capture images of analog gauges such as thermometers and pressure gauges. The captured images are analyzed by AI models to read the gauge scales and convert them into numerical data. The acquired data is stored in the cloud and can be accessed through a user interface or integrated with other systems.

Key Features:

  • Real-time data acquisition and analysis
  • Easy operation through user interface
  • Data visualization and report generation
  • Cloud storage for data
  • System integration through APIs

Okayama University Graduate School of Natural Science and Technology

Electric Energy and System Control Laboratory

Period: Apr 2013 - Mar 2015

Research
  • Apr 2013 - Mar 2015: Research on Energy Management Methods for Residential Distributed Power Generation Systems Using Solar Power and Fuel Cell Vehicles
    • Background
      • Recently, renewable energy implementation has been promoted as a measure against global warming.
      • Distributed power sources like solar power and fuel cells are expected to contribute to CO2 emission reduction.
      • However, distributed power sources have large output variations that can negatively impact power systems.
      • Therefore, an Energy Management System (EMS) is necessary for effective utilization of distributed power sources.
    • Research Overview
      • Proposed and verified the effectiveness of a new energy management method for residential distributed power generation systems using fuel cell vehicles and solar power.
      • The proposed method can be applied to both normal daily operation and emergency operation.
    • Proposed Method
      • In normal daily operation, power can be supplied from the power grid to household power loads. In this case, the proposed method is evaluated based on power fluctuation suppression rate and daily operating cost.
      • In emergency operation, power cannot be supplied from the power grid to household power loads. Therefore, in this case, the proposed method is evaluated by the number of operable days.
    • Results
      • The proposed method was found to suppress 40% of power fluctuations and reduce operating costs in normal daily operation. In emergency operation, the system can operate until power grid recovery.
    • International Conference Paper and Presentation
      • Paper Title: "Energy management method for residential distributed generation system using photovoltaic cells and fuel cell vehicle"
      • Authors: Shunsuke Sakae; Akiko Takahashi; Jun Imai; Shigeyuki Funabiki
      • Publisher: IEEE
      • Publication Year: 2014
      • Conference: 2014 IEEE Industry Application Society Annual Meeting
      • Paper URL: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/author/37085512035
